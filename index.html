Project Yin-Yang for SMT Solver Testing

Satisfiability Modulo Theory (SMT) solvers are fundamental tools for many computer science areas, such as verification, programming languages and software engineering. However, effectively testing SMT solvers was a long-standing and challenging problem. We propose two techniques for effectively testing SMT solvers: Semantic Fusion [1] and Type-aware Mutation [2]. The key idea of Semantic Fusion is to fuse two existing equisatisfiable (i.e., both satisfiable or unsatisfiable) formulas into a new formula that preserves the satisfiability by construction. Type-aware Mutation stress-tests SMT solvers by generating diverse type-correct formulas for differential testing. YinYang and OpFuzz, the realizations of Semantic Fusion and Type-aware Mutation, have shown their effectiveness by finding hundreds of bugs in the two state-of-the-art SMT solvers Z3 and CVC4. The long-term goal of this project is to explore novel, effective and practical techniques to help SMT solvers become more reliable and stable.
